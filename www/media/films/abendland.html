Abendland takes us behind the scenes of European nightlife -- into a multitude of nighttime settings from a rave, to a hospital, to a porn-shoot, to an airport bathroom. Europe is presented as a fragmented yet cohesive whole, bound together by machine technology. Surveillance is the motif that ties together a variety of seemingly unrelated situations. Director Geyrhalter pays particular attention to London street cameras, meticulously monitored by a team of government employees. Voyeuristic shots are not limited to acts of deviance or crime; rather, the camera shows us people and places that cinematic representations tend to miss. Abendland is a self-reflexive collage examining the complex ways in which media communications help to ensure national security. @geyrhalterfilm

About the Director



Nikolaus Geyrhalter was born in Vienna, Austria in 1972. In 1994, he founded his own production company titled Nikolaus Geyrhalter Filmproduktion (NGF). His award-winning works include Das Jahr nach Dayton (1997), Pripyat (1999), Elsewhere (2001), and Our Daily Bread (2005).
