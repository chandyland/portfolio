Suspiciously in Love

Directed by Sławomir Kryński; Poland | 2013 | 95 min.
March 23rd, 5:00pm at Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Ave.



A shy young man named Albert seeks love online and, while surfing the net, stumbles upon Basia. Each employs the same dating strategy-- Basia claims to be a mysterious woman named Simone while Albert poses as handsome French beau. The pair maintains this facade until their first date in "real life," at which point both send an attractive replacement to act on their behalf. However, this carefully orchestrated plan quickly unravels as the group is joined by a ludicrous gang of thieves hunting for a priceless diamonds collection. In this farcical comedy of errors, nobody knows who is in love and who is a suspect!

About the Director



Sławomir Kryński is one of Poland's most renowned directors and screenwriters. Toward the end of the 1970s, he studied filmmaking in Moscow and later transferred to the National Film School in Lodz. He later completed the director's program at this same school, where he now teaches. He has worked on many made-for-TV films, and he had his feature film debut in 1991 with the comedy, Child of Happiness (Silver Cluster Award in Lagow). His greatest film triumph was Great Book of Wishes (1997), which starred the distinguished actor Gustaw Holoubek and won numerous international awards, including the Golden Cluster.
